In the previous questions, we were asking you the following follow-up question:

'Fill in the blank: I think my answer has a __% chance of being right. A randomly drawn computer should answer for me if its accuracy is greater than that.'

Now we will practice how to answer this question correctly.

EXAMPLE

If you write down that your answer has a 58% chance of being right for this question, and the randomly-drawn computer is accurate 89% of the time, what will happen?

a. Your answer will be submitted, since the computer drawn has an accuracy higher than your threshold

b. The computer\'s answer will be submitted, since the computer drawn has an accuracy higher that your threshold

c. I am not sure

The answer to the above question is (b) because the computer has higher "confidence" in its answer being correct than you do.
